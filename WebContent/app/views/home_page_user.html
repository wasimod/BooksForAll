Georgics -(Latin)


by Virgil

description:The Georgics” (Gr: “Georgicon”) is a didactic poem, in the tradition of Hesiod, by the Roman poet Vergil (Virgil). It was Vergil’s second major work, published in 29 BCE, after “The Bucolics” (“Eclogues”), and the ostensible subject of the verses is rural life and farming. Part farming manual, part hymn of praise, part allegory, it contains some of Vergil's finest descriptive writing, with patriotic overtones and rich mythological allusions.
